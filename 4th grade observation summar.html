4th Grade Team: Observation Findings

Quarter 1 Summary & Proposed Next Steps

Glows: Areas of Strength

  • Positive Environment

    Students are consistently respectful and on-task with technology, creating a positive and productive learning atmosphere.

  • Lesson Design

    Evidence of student-friendly goals and planned scaffolds show intentional design to support diverse learners from the start.

  • Student Engagement

    Teachers are connecting learning to student interests and creating opportunities for collaboration, such as partner discussions.

Grows: Opportunities for Growth

  • Student Agency

    Increase student ownership of norms and provide meaningful choices in how they learn and demonstrate understanding.

  • Technology Integration

    Shift from technology as a consumption tool (watching videos) to a creation and collaboration tool (analyzing data, creating content).

  • Feedback & Revision

    Develop feedback that is specific and actionable, guiding students on *how* to revise and improve their work, beyond just correctness.

Next Steps: A Partnership for Access

  • Co-Planning & Co-Teaching

    Partner to design and model lessons that integrate technology for creation and collaboration, based on team goals.

  • Targeted Professional Learning

    Organize workshops on high-priority topics like offering student choice, fostering digital collaboration, and providing actionable feedback.

  • Focus on UDL

    Collaboratively apply the Universal Design for Learning framework to proactively remove barriers and increase access for all students.
